# Phase 0 — Wix Content Audit

**Site reviewed:** <https://darringtonfield.wixsite.com/darrington-village>  
**Review date:** 16 July 2026  
**Method:** Direct browser inspection of every page in the Wix navigation

---

## 1. Summary

The Wix site contains five public pages:

1. Home
2. Feast & Fayre
3. 5 Mile Run
4. Bonfire & Fireworks
5. Contact Us

It provides useful source material for the new website, including the Trust's purpose, recent investment in the field, event descriptions, contact details and current social profiles.

The content should be preserved and rewritten rather than copied verbatim. The Wix site is no longer maintained as the current public source, so its event information may be out of date. Several statements require confirmation, and the current structure omits Santa Special, detailed field information, accessibility, governance, privacy and volunteer guidance.

## 4. Homepage audit

### Existing content

The homepage explains:

- many residents do not know who owns and maintains the field;
- the field is not owned or funded by Wakefield Council, the Parish Council or the Church;
- it is owned/managed through the registered Darrington Village Field Trust;
- the field was acquired for the community in the 1970s;
- it has three trustees;
- day-to-day management is organised by a committee including village organisations and members of the public;
- events help fund the field;
- 2024 work included repair and replacement of play equipment costing more than £72,000;
- the main gate and posts were replaced and moved at a cost of more than £3,000;
- events, donations and local government support made this possible; and
- financial help, skills and expertise are welcomed.

The page is signed by Richard Hirst, Chair.

### Trustees named on Wix

- Richard Hirst
- Pete Batty
- Nick Storr

The Charity Commission currently lists the formal names Richard Hirst, Peter Thomas Batty and Nicholas Charles Storr. The Trust should choose the approved public display form while keeping formal governance references accurate.

### Recommendation

Split this content across:

- homepage introduction;
- About the Trust;
- field improvements/impact;
- Get Involved; and
- governance.

The clarification about ownership is valuable and should be retained prominently, but expressed positively and concisely.

### Verification required

- Exact acquisition date/history.
- Exact 2024 project costs.
- Whether local-government support may be described more specifically.
- Current committee structure and which members should be named.
- Whether the Trust wants the Chair's signed message retained.

## 6. Five Mile Run audit

### Existing content

- The run precedes the annual Feast & Fayre.
- The route is described as undulating.
- It starts in Darrington, heads to Carleton and Wentbridge, then returns to finish on the Village Field.
- The Wix wording welcomes people who run, jog or walk.
- Entry includes Feast & Fayre admission, bag drop, water station, finisher medal and category cash prizes.
- Entry uses an external Bookitzone form: <https://bookitzone.com/stephen_berry_1/NAjFFX>
- No entries are accepted on the day.
- The stated limit is 250 participants.
- Contact email on the Contact page: `darrington5mile@email.com`.

### Critical verification

The current organiser must confirm:

- 2026 date and start time;
- whether walkers are permitted;
- age requirements;
- route and road closures;
- entry limit;
- no-on-the-day-entry rule;
- Bookitzone link and closing date;
- entry fee;
- included benefits;
- transfers/refunds;
- bag-drop arrangements;
- prize categories;
- accessibility and suitability; and
- current contact address.

Old race guides must not override current organiser instructions.

### Disposition

Preserve the visitor-friendly route summary, but build the page around verified registration, safety and practical information.

## 7. Bonfire & Fireworks audit

### Existing content

- The page celebrates the 2025 event.
- It mentions the bonfire and fireworks display, BBQ, hot chocolate including an adult version, refreshments and children's entertainment.
- It says the 2026 date and further details will be announced in the coming weeks.
- Gate and refreshment proceeds are described as funding the following year's fireworks.

### Date confirmation and Wix discrepancy

The prototype states:

- Saturday 7 November 2026;
- gates at 5:00pm; and
- several practical and safety arrangements.

Laurie confirmed on 16 July 2026 that **Saturday 7 November 2026** is the correct Bonfire Night date. The Wix page is out of date because it is not being maintained as the current public source.

The prototype's 5:00pm gates time and other operational arrangements remain to be confirmed separately.

### Disposition

- **Keep:** community atmosphere, fundraising cycle and 2025 retrospective.
- **Rewrite:** promotional language.
- **Confirmed:** Saturday 7 November 2026.
- **Verify:** opening time and all other 2026 operational details.
- **Add:** booking/price, timings, parking, accessibility, safety, dogs, weather, toilets and FAQ.
