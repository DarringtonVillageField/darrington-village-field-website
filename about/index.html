The Village Field Trust

Caring for the Field, together

The registered charity responsible for protecting, maintaining and improving Darrington Village Field for the benefit of the community.

Our purpose

A field held for public benefit

Darrington Village Field is cared for through Darrington Village Field Trust—not by the local council, Parish Council or Church.

The Trust’s purpose is to provide and maintain the playing field and play equipment for public use in Darrington. It works to keep the space welcoming today while protecting it for future generations.

Our story

Rooted in village history

Darrington Village Field was acquired for the community in the 1970s, with Darrington Village Field Trust registered as a charity on 13 January 1977.

That same year was Queen Elizabeth II’s Silver Jubilee and, in celebration, the community came together to hold a fayre on the Field, with people from the village dressed in Edwardian costumes. The event helped inspire what became the Feast & Fayre, with the Edwardian Lady remaining a symbol of what community spirit can create.

We continue to grow memories of the Field’s history, so if you have any photographs, documents or memories that you can share, feel free to contact the Trust and we can look to add them to our heritage.
